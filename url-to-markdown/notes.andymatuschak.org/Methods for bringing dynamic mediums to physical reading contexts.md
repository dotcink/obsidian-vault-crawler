# Methods for bringing dynamic mediums to physical reading contexts

One response to [[Reading texts on computers is unpleasant]] is to give up, to invert the problem: how to bring the properties of dynamic mediums to physical books?

- Projectors / cameras (a la [[Dynamicland]])
	- I like the notion of a wide-angle camera you can clip onto a book, just like you might clip on a little light for reading at night.
- Treat a phone or tablet as a “companion device”—it’s not where the reading happens, but it’s perhaps where dynamic supports appear.
- [[Voice-based interface]]
	- [[Specifying book passages with a few spoken words]]

Specifying book passages with a few spoken words

Voice-based interface offers one avenue for Methods for bringing dynamic mediums to physical reading contexts. Many dynamic interactions involving a text will require you to indicate a passage of interest in the text, or to know what you’re looking at. For instance, maybe you’d like to make a note about a passage, or highlight it, or add a memory system prompt about it. How to do that with voice?
